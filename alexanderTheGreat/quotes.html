The Greatest Quotes From Alexander the Great

"I am not afraid of an army of lions led by a sheep; I am afraid of an army of sheep led by a lion."

This quote emphasizes the importance of leadership over the individual capabilities of team members. Alexander suggests that even a group of less formidable individuals (the sheep) can become a formidable force under strong leadership (the lion). It reflects the value he placed on leadership qualities and strategy in warfare.

"There is nothing impossible to him who will try."

This quote speaks to Alexander's ambitious nature and his belief in the power of determination and effort. It reflects an optimistic view that challenges can be overcome with persistence and effort, a mindset that was likely crucial in his extensive military campaigns.

"How should a man be capable of grooming his own horse, or of furbishing his own spear and helmet, if he allows himself to become unaccustomed to tending even his own person, which is his most treasured belonging?"

This quote highlights the importance of self-care and personal discipline. Alexander points out that taking care of one's own needs and well-being is the foundation for being able to take care of other responsibilities. This can be seen as a reflection of the holistic approach to leadership and personal development in ancient Greek philosophy.

"Oh! Most miserable wretch that I am! Why have I not learnt how to swim?"

This quote, somewhat humorously, reflects on the limitations of even the greatest leaders or warriors. It's a reminder that no one is capable of everything and that there are always new skills to learn and new areas in which to grow. This may also indicate a moment of self-reflection or humility from a figure often known for his confidence and ambition.
